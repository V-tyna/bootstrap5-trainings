How to propagate succulents?

The main ways of propagation succulents involve cutting and separating the leaves.

Usually succulents grow in arid climates. So, this mean that these plans have adaptive methods for moisture retention. Plants have thick, moisture-laden leaves or stems that contain a lot of vitality. Even small leaf strives to live and give yourself a continuation in a new plant. Most succulents such as Echeveria, Haworthia, Crassula, Sedum and some others are capable of leaf propagation. Some plants, for example Sanseveria, can reproduce even with part of a leaf.

In order to propagate a succulent with a leaf only, it is enough to carefully separate the leaf from the stem. The main thing is that the leaf is completely separated and its base is not damaged.

The second and fastest way to propagate is by cuttings. Having separated the stalk (part of the stem with leaves) or leaf, they must be left in a dry place until roots appear. With a leaf, it is better to wait not only for the roots, but also for the buds. Since not all leaves can give a new plant, even if they do have roots. After the roots appear, a stalk or leaf can be planted in the pot and watered a little.

I recommend watering the plant 2-3 days before any propagation of any of the above methods before the cutting or separating the leaves. So the plant will pick up moisture. And this means that when the cutting or leaf dries up, it will have a better chance of surviving, and therefore it will recover faster after the appearance of roots.
